# NTU EEE MSc Dissertation - Official Guidelines Reference

This document contains the complete NTU EEE formatting and content requirements extracted from the official "Guidelines for MSc Dissertations" document.

## FORMAT

### 1. General Requirements

- Typewritten in English, third person
- 1.5 or double spacing between lines
- No prescribed length; recommended 60 to 100 pages
- Font size 12, preferably printed double-sided
- Use your own words; do not copy from books/reports
- Standard International Units (SI) should be used

### 2. Paper

- White A4 size bond paper
- At least 80 g/m2 weight
- Applicable for hard copy requested by examiner

### 3. Margins

- Left-hand side: 3.5 cm
- Top, bottom, right-hand side: 3 cm

### 4. Typography

- Same type setting throughout the main text
- Letters must not vary in size, type, or shade
- Same font and pitch throughout, except for highlighting important matters

### 5. Spacing

- 1.5 line spacing for the text of the report

### 6. Page Count

- 60 to 100 printed pages
- Preferably printed double-sided
- Hard copy applicable when requested by examiner

## REQUIRED SECTIONS (in order)

1. **Title page**
2. **Statement of Originality**
3. **Supervisor Declaration Statement**
4. **Authorship Attribution Statement**
5. **Table of Contents**
6. **Abstract**
7. **Acknowledgement page** - recognition of advisory or financial assistance (optional)
8. **Acronyms** (optional)
9. **Symbols** (optional)
10. **List of Figures**
11. **List of Tables**
12. **Introductory chapter**
13. **Text chapters**
14. **References**
15. **Appendix/appendices** (optional)

## SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

- A softcopy of the dissertation and Turnitin Originality Report (HTML format) must be uploaded to the Dissertation Submission E-Form
- After examination and approval, students must submit a softcopy of the final version to the School through the supervisor and upload a copy to NTU Library (DR-NTU)
- Samples of cover page, title page, 3 declaration statements, and dissertation format are in Appendices 1 to 6 of the official guidelines

## STANDARD OF WRITING

- Writing should be logically laid out
- Grammatically correct, clear, and concise English
- If a word, phrase, or sentence can be deleted without changing the meaning, delete it
- Figures and tables numbered sequentially by chapter
- Each figure/table must have a caption
- Each must be referred to in the main text
- Figures/tables should appear near where they are referenced

## CONTENTS GUIDE

### Abstract

- Short and concise passage on important work and contributions
- Cover: motivation, problem pursued, method employed, results obtained, significant achievements
- Should NOT contain peripheral things like summary of literature review
- Do not say "a certain issue has been studied without stating results"
- Generally one page is the right length

### Chapter 1: Introduction

- Lead readers into the problem
- Set the scene with background and motivation
- State objectives and scope of the project
- Conclude with the layout of the dissertation

### Chapter 2: Literature Review

- What has been done before on the problem
- Shows awareness of related work beyond narrow focus
- Some prior work might be instructive for solving the problem
- Explain why you are taking your chosen direction

### Chapters 3-5: Technical Chapters

- Fundamental theories relevant to the solution
- Supporting technologies needed for implementation
- The solution itself
- Results and analysis of results

### Chapter 6: Conclusion

Three parts:
1. **Summary** - concise summary of work done (similar to abstract)
2. **Conclusion** - highlight significance of results, consequences, critical assessment
3. **Recommendations and/or Future Work** - identify inadequacies, suggest how gaps may be filled

### Chapter Count

- Generally no more than 6 or 7 chapters
- If more, consider merging some chapters

## SUPPLEMENTARY ELEMENTS

### References

- List of sources the writer quotes directly, takes original ideas from, and refers to
- IEEE style is the accepted citation method
- Most widely accepted way to cite specific sources in scientific/technical reports

### Appendix

- Related data not necessary for immediate understanding of the discussion
- May contain: tables, graphs, illustrations, equipment descriptions, forms, data sheets, questionnaires, equations, and any material for record purposes
- Each entry requires an identifying title
- Every entry must be referred to in the body of the report
- Each appendix must be lettered, beginning with Appendix A
- List of appendices should appear in the table of contents following the references entry
